Owlie


Decision Making Simplified.





About


Owlie is an AI based Facebook Messenger app that simplifies user’s day-to-day decision-making problems. User just has to feed in the question he wants to ask to his friends, Owlie will generate interactive polls that can be sent to friends via Facebook Messenger.



My Role


Concept developementConceiving the idea, User interviews and Surveys, Market Research, Business Model development.

Product designProduct Roadmap, User flow, Wireframe and prototype of the app, UI design, User testing of the app, Brand design, Messenger bot flow, Website design and development


Achievements


1. Filed a patent application as an Inventor in “Context Analysis of Message Enhancement”

2. Looking at the quality of product and problem that we are trying to solve, Facebook funded the project through the Bootstrap FbStart Program.


Problem Statement


Some people are good at making decisions in their lives while others are not. Some take help of internet while some need help from their friends, family, acquaintances etc. Depending on the type of the decision, some dig into the matter and carefully analysis before taking the decision while others don’t.


Presently, millennials have many ways that help them to solve their day to day decision making problems — like friends, family, internet, few mobile apps. But unfortunately, either these applications have poor user experience, ‘another app to download’ problem or solved problems specific to only one category like politics, fashion industry etc. Similarly, brands have no straightforward approach to reach to their consumers for their product reviews to reach to better decision making for their products or services.


Messaging apps like Facebook Messenger, WhatsApp, LINE, KakaoTalk etc. are used for having such conversations. But the response rate to the advice from peers on these messaging apps decreases due to these factors:


No Response Aggregation

Inefficient Process — cluttered UI, typing hassle, deviation from topic, long time to conclude etc.

App Design


The design was supposed to come out to be high-end design something like Uber, which is acceptable by all demography. Along with that, it needs to be highly interactive and fluid with easiest and simplest way to create and answer polls. Other inspirations are: Paper (Facebook), Twitter Moments, Snapchat and Medium.
